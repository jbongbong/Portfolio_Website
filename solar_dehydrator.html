Project Description

We aim to reduce food waste in Singapore. Linking this to our DES 2D content, we are required to create a food dryer system.

Inspired by solar cookers, a parabolic reflector system employs a mirror sheet laid on a parabolic trough to form a focal line that incident sunlight concentrates on. A cooker tube is laid along the focal line, causing the sunlight to shine directly onto the cooker and heat the tube to 60째C-80째C. While a solar cooker is fully enclosed to allow heat to accumulate and cook the food inside, our system is an open system that draws in outside air. This air is warmed by the tube to 20째C-30째C above ambient temperature and directed over the food, thus continuously removing moisture from the food. The parabolic reflector is much cheaper as it uses solar energy to heat air to optimal drying temperatures and only requires solar tracking on 1-axis, while the desiccant system relies on electricity, or a 2-axis solar tracker if using lens, to dry its desiccant.
